China has long relied on coal-fired power plants that have suffocated the country. But it is now taking the lead in clean energy.


The Chinese National Energy Administration confirmed its plan to invest at least US$360 billion on renewable energy development such as solar and wind this early year, which would also create about 13 million jobs in the renewable energy sector by 2020.


Not only does China strive for the domestic renewable market, but it also focuses on foreign investment.


The latest report from Institute for Energy Economics and Financial Analysis (IEEFA) indicated that China increased its foreign investment in renewables by 60 per cent to reach a record US$32 billion in 2016 and is expected to pick up the pace this year.
